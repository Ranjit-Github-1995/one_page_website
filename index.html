About Us

I first became familiar with massage during my time in the far east, where I experienced a number of different approaches. Although I was always impressed by the sense of wellbeing I received after a relaxation massage, I was disappointed that the only option was to close my eyes.

As a keen scuba diver I already knew how relaxing floating over tropical coral reefs could be. Part of the appeal of the underwater world is the way in which marine life continues around the observer, without shying away, one of the few environments in which one can truly immerse oneself. With the development of 3D screen and camera technology it has become possible to bring this sensation to the massage table, giving a more relaxing and fascinating experience.

I trained in Swedish Massage, the prominent relaxation technique and Oncology Massage. Recent research has revealed the benefits of massage to people living with cancer and the positive influence it could have on their quality of life and outcomes. I have combined my qualifications in these techniques with the technological innovation so that I could bring the 3D immersive massage experience to cancer patients.

Our Video

During the first half of the massage you will lie face down, watching the screen below, while you journey over the unspoilt reefs of Raja Ampat in Papua, swimming amongst colourful corals and beautiful fish. Subtle blue lighting surrounds you while calming rhythms of traditional Indonesian Gamelan music play in the background.

For the second half of your massage, lying on your back, a screen above you takes you to the beautiful temples of Bali where a traditional Legung dance is performed. The treatment culminates with a neck and face massage, using only cold pressed organic oils, and hot towel revitalisation.
